# Otter.ai Application — Short Answer + LinkedIn Outreach

## "Describe an insight you generated and what product impact that insight had on the product strategy or product roadmap."

At Sofy.ai, I analyzed weekly test execution data across 7 Microsoft product teams and identified that a disproportionate share of regression failures were clustering in two specific feature areas (Endpoint DLP and Auto-labeling) that had been treated as low-risk in sprint planning. I surfaced this pattern through a recurring dashboard I built, which led the product leads to reallocate QA coverage and delay a release that would have shipped with undetected regressions — ultimately reducing post-release defect rates for those features and reshaping how the team prioritized regression testing in subsequent sprint cycles.

### Why these work (delete before sending):

1. **Opens with the personal connection** — "since we worked together" establishes you're not a stranger cold-messaging. That's your biggest advantage.
2. **States what you want clearly** — no beating around the bush. Recruiters and employees both respect directness.
3. **Gives her a reason to say yes** — you briefly summarize why you're a fit so she can feel confident referring you. She doesn't have to guess whether you're qualified.
4. **Gives her an out** — "totally understand if the timing isn't right" removes social pressure. Counterintuitively, this makes people MORE likely to say yes.
5. **Doesn't dump your resume in the message** — that's transactional. If she says yes, then you send it. Let her opt in first.
6. **Ends warm** — "love to catch up" reminds her you're a human, not just someone networking at her.
